What MedRelay Actually Does

Transparent capabilities ‚Äî no black boxes.

1. Document Processing

  • Reads PDF, DOCX, and TXT files
  • Assesses extraction quality (OCR detection)
  • Flags low-quality scans for manual review
  • Detects multiple patients in single documents

2. Clinical Information Extraction

  • Patient demographics (age, NHS number)
  • Symptom duration with temporal categories
  • Risk factors (smoking, alcohol)
  • Clinical entities with SNOMED codes

3. Guideline Checking

  • 60+ rules based on NICE NG12 & CG103
  • Duration thresholds (e.g., 3-week hoarseness)
  • Age thresholds (e.g., 45+ for 2WW)
  • Red flag combinations

4. Negation Handling

  • "No stridor" does NOT trigger emergency
  • 40+ negation patterns recognised
  • Handles medical abbreviations (-ve, r/o)
  • Context-aware terminators

5. Temporal Validation

  • 5 clinical categories (hyperacute to chronic)
  • Conflict detection ("sudden" vs "6 months")
  • NICE duration threshold checking
  • Confidence adjustment for ambiguity

6. Dual Validation

  • Deterministic rules run first (safety net)
  • AI provides confidence scoring
  • Rules always override AI suggestions
  • Full audit trail for every decision

But Is AI Safe for Clinical Work?

Let us be very clear: the AI does not make decisions. Clinicians do. MedRelay is a transparent assistant, not a replacement for clinical judgment.

You See Everything

Every piece of information the AI extracted. Every rule it checked. Every reason for its suggestion. No black boxes. Full transparency.

Rules Override AI

Hard-coded clinical rules (based on NICE guidelines) always take precedence. If a red flag is present, it's flagged ‚Äî regardless of what the AI model thinks.

Uncertainty = Human Review

Low confidence? Conflicting signals? Unusual presentation? These go straight to a clinician for review. No auto-routing of edge cases.

Unknown = Unknown

If no rules match, the system returns "Unknown" priority ‚Äî not "Routine". Atypical presentations are flagged for specialist review.
